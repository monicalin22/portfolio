Gazer

For this competition, Adobe + Netflix tasked us with creating a third-party website that would bring people closer together when streaming movies. My partner and I decided to create a platform that would allow users to simulate in-person viewing experiences while also capitalizing on activities you can only do online. Our product, Gazer, (with a name inspired by the endless viewing of the galaxy, ) allows users to view content in revolutionary ways.

Interactive Prototype

Analysis of Key Features

Home Screen

Users can view suggested theatres and movies on their dashboard. Each movie has categories, ratings, and bookmarks. A virutal assistant, Alex, is available at the bottom for easy room creation and navigation.

Private Rooms

Users can customize rooms with emojis and decriptions and invite their friends to join. Moreover, Alex can help users schedule streaming times! (This demo is from the tour.)

Learning Goals

The first thing that appears in a user's recommendations is content that matches their "learning goals." This feature is meant to boost awareness and help the user stay educated regarding topics they are interested in.

Streaming

Users can view content with others while videochatting and messaging! On top of that, they can save interesting facts and react with emojis to scenes that make them feel expressive.

My Moments

When you react to certain scenes, these moments will be saved and organized by emojis. Hovering over each scene plays it, and users can share these saved moments with a unique link.

Our goal was to simulate the social aspects of in-person streaming while also leveraging social interactions unique to online platforms.

Research

Brief Interviews

We conducted quick interviews with 6 people in total to ask about their social viewing experiences.
We identifed a couple of pain points:

  • People have a difficult time deciding on movies with their friends in a timely manner.
  • It is hard to access movie reviews quickly without Googling anything.
  • It is difficult to find new content.
  • Viewers find it hard to decide on a single movie that everyone in a group will like.
  • Text chatting during a movie is not the most user-friendly way to go about interactions.

Affinity Wall

We created an affinity map to see trends in experiences. We found that currently, there was not a good platform that synced streaming experiences and that streaming is more about the social interactions than it is about watching the movie or show (most of the time.)

Competitive Research

We did researched the UI/UX of 12 "similar" applications. Below are a few summaries.

  • Netflix Party: Super limited in capabilities. Only allows for chatting, and the chat box cannot be minimized. Customizable "profiles."
  • Spotify: Can view what friends just listened to via "Friend Activity." Feels more like a dashboard.
  • Facebook Rooms & Live: Can create private chat rooms for activities with friends. Facebook Live has a feature where you can chat, view content, and spam with emojis for quick reactions.
  • r/BestofNetflix: Forum where anyone can discuss a movie with others.

Ideation

Defining the Audience

Before moving forward, we did a bit more research regarding the main consumers of content. We decided that our application would be all-inclusive, though the design would be a bit more modern and hip. Some features may also seem like they are better suited for younger audiences, but they are not discriminatory towards older audiences.

Moodboard & Branding

Information Architecture

Next, we solidified the features of our application. Our information architecture diagram ties all of the features together and gives a glimpse into Gazer's user flow. On Gazer, the user would create an account then go through an onboarding process to choose their favorite content genres, aided by Alex, the app's virtual assistant. (We also added a more extensive tour for the judges since they probably have a really short time with each entry and would rather be guided through the app than figure things out for themselves.)
